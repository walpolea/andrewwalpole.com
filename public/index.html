What I've Learned

  • Start with vision and strategy. You can’t establish a direction without knowing your destination. Having a strategy is essential, but it can’t be comprehensive, so embrace the unknown and focus on moving in the right direction; the path will present itself.

  • Doing something new or different is hard. Just take action. Forget about success or failure. You can’t blaze a trail without a few missteps and course corrections, and learning where not to go is invaluable.

  • The details matter. A lot. But don’t expect to understand them up-front. Start high and zoom in until things get clear.

  • You can’t tell people to change. First you have to motivate them by selling them why. Then inspire them by showing them how. If you do it right, you don’t even have to tell them what’s changing.

  • Solving a problem and knowing the answer are two very different things. Always be skeptical about the things you know and get excited about the prospect of being wrong.
